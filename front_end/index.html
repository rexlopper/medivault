What We Are Validating

  • Patients struggle to remember and communicate medically relevant information.
  • Doctors lose time reconstructing history during already compressed consultations.
  • Records stay fragmented across paper, chat threads, isolated EMRs, and memory.
5+ stakeholder interviews synthesized
3 care layers studied: barangay, municipal, provincial

Problem Landscape

We are not positioning a product yet. We are making the pain visible.

01

Patients carry critical health data in memory

Diagnoses, medications, allergies, lab results, and timelines are often recalled under pressure, incompletely, or not at all.

02

Consultations are overloaded moments

Doctors are interviewing, thinking clinically, and documenting at the same time. Attention splits. Quality drops. Important context gets lost.

03

Records do not move cleanly across settings

Paper folders, verbal handoffs, referral chat groups, and siloed systems create care journeys where continuity depends on luck.

04

Public health facilities are stretched thin

Understaffing, congestion, limited trust in lower-level facilities, and exhausted staff turn every operational gap into a clinical problem.

Research Focus

Our work is centered on field insight, not feature assumptions.

Who we are listening to

  • Patients navigating real consultations
  • Doctors in private, hospital, and public settings
  • Provincial and municipal health stakeholders
  • Legal and policy experts around privacy and public implementation

What we are mapping

  • Where patient information becomes incomplete, delayed, or distorted
  • How documentation burdens affect care quality and workflow
  • What current workarounds reveal about unmet needs
  • How trust, staffing, and infrastructure shape adoption realities

Current Position

MediVault is in active validation.
